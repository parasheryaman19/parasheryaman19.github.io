Project Associate - Developing high capacity optical multiuser/multichannel systems & networks

August 2018 - August 2019 Optical Communication Systems Photonic Devices Optical Networks

At the lab at Delhi Technological University under the guidance of Prof. Dr. Gurjit Kaur, I mainly explore the use of signal processing, communication theory, and optical techniques in designing high capacity optical multiuser/multichannel systems and networks that are capable of extending transmission distances, increase bandwidth, and reduce energy consumption.

 

Figure: Members of the group with Prof. Dr. Gurjit Kaur(first from the left)

During my wonderful time with the group, I have worked on several book chapters related to Integrated Optics (Photonic Integrated Circuits), Advanced & Hybrid Optical Communication Systems, Free Space Optics , Spatial Division Multiplexing, Ultradense Wavelength Division Multiplexing, Machine Learning, Big Data, Embedded Systems, IoT, along with a conference paper and journal paper in Springer, Elsevier, CRC, IGI Global, OSA, etc.

 

Figure: My workstation at DTU

Apart from this, I was also involved in the design, optimization, and application of photonic devices for both linear and nonlinear optical interactions.

About Research Group

The current research of the group is focused on these directions: Adaptive optical networks, Advanced modulation and coding for optical communication systems, FSO and hybrid RF/FSO networks and communications, Design and modeling of optoelectronics, nano-photonics devices, such as modulators, LEDs, lasers, and photodetectors, on Si substrate, and Green Technology for IoT & Smart Cities applications.
